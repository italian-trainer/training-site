Host Sequence of Service (SOS)

4. Alerting the Server

Before you head back to the host stand, it’s good to let the server you just seated know that they have a new table. Usually they’re around their section anyway, and simple eye contact or making sure they see you as you’re seating the guests is enough. Other times you may need to make a pass by the POS or through the expo line to let them know.

This helps the servers make a plan for getting around to their new tables, as soon as possible, and in really busy circumstances may be an opportunity for them to let you know there a little behind, so it might be best to wait a bit before seating them again.

On some shifts there may be tables that aren’t pre-assigned to servers. Those are called “pick-up” tables, and they need to be assigned as they’re seated. You’ll work with the manager throughout the shift to determine which server should pick up the tables that are seated in the pick-up section. Your manager might give you some direction at the start of the shift, or they may give you direction as you go. If you have to seat a pick- up table that you haven’t already been given some direction for, your next stop should be the manager.

5. Thank the Guest

The most important words our guests can hear from you are “Thank you.”

Just like you are most often the first person our guests see when they come through the door, you will very often also be the last Maria’s team member they pass on the way out the door.

Letting each guest know they were appreciated puts the finishing touch on the quality of their experience, and it’s just one more thing you can do to be of service and help make Maria’s the friendliest family restaurant in the neighborhood.

If you can, open the door for them, ask how everything was and invite them to come back, soon. The warm and accommodating service people have come to expect from Maria’s Italian Kitchen means going out of our way for every guest from the time they arrive to the time they leave. Saying “thank you” goes a long way, and paired with a genuine smile, it can leave our guests with a positive final impression of us on their way home.
