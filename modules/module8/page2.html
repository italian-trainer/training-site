Server: Sequence of Service Part 3

16. Serve Dessert and Coffee (within 2-3 minutes)

Once the dessert order is in, it should be ready to serve quickly. Don’t forget to consider any fresh silverware or share plates the guests will need, and if you can, get that to the table ahead of time.

When delivering dessert or coffee to the table, you should follow all the same steps as delivering any course. Announce the items you serving and double check to ensure that everyone has everything they need.

17. Present Check

The check should be presented as soon as desserts are on the table, or, if desserts are declined, as soon after you’ve cleared the table as possible. Checks should be placed face up in a check presenter and the presenter should be placed as near as possible to the host, if one can be identified. If not, simply place the check near the middle of the table.

“I’ll just leave this here, and I’ll take it when you’re ready.”

Once you’ve dropped the check, keep an eye out for the guests to be ready to pay so that you can retrieve it and run the payment as quickly as possible
