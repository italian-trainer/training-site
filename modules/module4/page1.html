Professional Service

Being a great server is about a lot more than just taking people’s orders and bringing them food. Service is about providing for the guests’ needs, making them feel comfortable and well taken care of, and doing it seamlessly and efficiently.

The server has many roles which can be summed up as follows:
#1 Role: To sell
#2 Role: To service
#3 Role: To satisfy
#4 Role: To see them again

Be prepared

Being prepared starts with showing up on time, in uniform and ready to work. “Ready to work” means physically and mentally. You have to be focused on the task at hand and bring a positive attitude. It also means double-checking that the restaurant is set and ready to go, especially, your own station.

Be friendly and make eye contact

Professional service takes personality. Always make eye contact with guests to let them know you are listening and focused on them.

Consolidate

One of the biggest mistakes new servers make is trying to get things done one piece at a time. When you’re busy that can get overwhelming. To be efficient and get everything done in a timely fashion, you have to consolidate. Instead of taking care of everything one table needs and then checking on the next, it works better to work your whole section like one big table. Walk through and check on everybody, then go do everything you can and bring back whatever you need for everyone all at once.
