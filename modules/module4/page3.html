Ringing Orders and Timing Courses

Once a table has given you an order, the next step is to get it immediately rung in, so that the food gets out to your guests as soon as possible.

As you ring orders in, you need to make sure you are ringing according to seat number, and considering the timing of multiple courses, if you have any.

Timing Courses

As a general rule, you want entrées to arrive at the table just about the time your guests are finishing with their first course. If your table is sharing an appetizer, it’s probably ok if the entrées arrive before the appetizer is completely gone, but if you have four guests all having their own salad to start, you don’t want the entrée’s to arrive when they’re only halfway through those salads.

It’s important to remember that as soon as you send a ticket to the kitchen, it immediately goes into the line to be prepared and sent out to the table, as soon as it’s ready. In most instances with multiple courses, you’ll probably be able to ring your first course and send it, followed immediately by ringing and sending the entrées and the timing is likely to work out.

But occasionally, you may find that you need to use the HOLD function in the POS, which allows you to ring your items in without immediately sending the ticket to the kitchen, so that you can you can better control the timing.
