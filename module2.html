Being "Of Service"

A big part of being a member of the Maria’s team is demonstrating an “of service” attitude to all of our guests. Every guest is your guest and it is part of your role to help us create the welcoming, friendly, “of service” atmosphere that we want guests to remember every time they think of Maria’s.

But what does being “of service” mean? What do we really expect to see?

Being “of service” means always putting yourself in the position to improve the Maria’s experience for every guest you see. It means taking the extra step and adding the little touches that really set the Maria’s experience apart, and above the rest. It means doing it as a matter of personal pride and habit.

You may have heard people refer to the idea of “going the extra mile,” and that’s a big part of what being “of service” is about, but it’s more than that.

Being “of service” is not just in occasional specific actions where you did a little bit more. It’s an all-the-time attitude that defines your treatment of our guests. It’s a demeanor and a culture of service that you carry with you.

The “of service” attitude can show up in a lot of ways:

  • It’s never being bothered by a guest’s request, even if they aren’t your guest

  • It’s keeping an eye out for the needs of guests, even when they aren’t seated in your section

  • It’s walking guests toward the restroom, rather than simply pointing across the dining room

  • It’s opening the door for guests, both entering and leaving the restaurant, anytime you can

  • It’s a constant smile, eye contact and verbal acknowledgement

  • It’s keeping the kids happy by offering to get their food out as quickly as you can

  • It’s anything you reasonably can do to make the guest experience better

Being “of service” means just that: being “of service” .

And it doesn’t stop with your guests. You can, and should, be “of service” to the other members of your team because that’s what being a good team member means.

In the end, being “of service” is about developing and demonstrating your pride and a culture of service and Teamwork. All day. Every day.

Teamwork

Teamwork is the way everything gets done. No one can do it all alone, so making sure our guests have the experience they deserve and expect means everyone working together and helping out as a team.

You should be proactive and always asking yourself “what else can I do to help?” Don’t just stand around and wait to be asked. There is always something to do. A true team player asks for help when they need it and offers help to others who look like they might be overwhelmed. Extend this support to your Teammates in other areas of your restaurant; Cashiers, Bussers, Line Cooks, Hosts, etc…

At Maria’s, every guest is your guest. Even if you aren’t directly responsible for that guest, at that moment, you want to ensure they have a positive experience so that they’ll come back again and again. It’s repeat business that really makes our restaurants successful.

All our team members are ambassadors of MIK always welcoming and smiling to all of our guests. We adopt the practice of making eye contact with all our guests, not just “my guests”. We live the golden rule: “All for one and one for all”, not “my” but rather “our” guests.

Phone Etiquette

We all answer the phone at Maria’s Italian Kitchen. Yes, we have cashiers and managers who may be primarily responsible for answering the phone, but that doesn’t mean we can’t all do it. The cashier may be on another call or dealing with a guest who has arrived to pick up their order. Helping out is part of good teamwork and guest service, and all calls should to be answered within three rings. Treat the phone as a guest, every guest is your guest; every table is your table mentality!

Answering the Phone

“Hello, thank you for choosing Maria’s Italian Kitchen in Sherman Oaks, this is Jason. How can I help you?”

This simple verbiage is perfect to use when answering the phone every time

  • It thanks the guest for choosing Maria’s

  • It identifies not just that they’ve called Maria’s, but the specific location

  • It gives the guest your name, which personalizes your assistance

  • It offers your help

Once you’ve greeted the caller listen carefully to what it is they need so you can help them appropriately. If you aren’t sure about the answer, don’t guess. Just politely ask them to hold while you find that answer.

“Let me check on that for you. Can you hold for just one moment?”

What if the caller wants to place an order for pick-up or delivery?

Maria’s Italian Kitchen does a lot of take-out and delivery business, so when those calls come in, you need to know where to direct them. Direct all pickup and delivery orders to Cashiers or the Manager on duty.

What if the caller has questions about catering?

Catering from Maria’s is also very popular, so if the caller wants to know if we offer catering, the answer is “yes.” However, answering questions about catering, or taking a catering order, is very different than taking an order from our regular menu, so its best if the cashier or a manager handles those calls too.

If the guest isn’t looking to talk to anyone right away, you can direct them to our website which has lots of catering information and let them know they can call back anytime if they have more questions.

What if the caller wants to make a reservation?

At Maria’s we only take reservations for parties of 7 or more, and the rest of our seating is offered on a “first come, first seated” basis. When a guest asks about making a reservation you need to get a little bit more information.

“Ok, we generally only take reservations for larger parties. How many are in your group?”

If the number is seven or more:
“Ok, absolutely. Let me have you talk to my manager, Mark, and he can make that reservation for you. Can you hold for just one moment?”

Reservations should always be handled by a manager, in case there are any special considerations or arrangements that need to be made.

If the number is six or fewer: “Ok, well we only take reservations for parties of seven or more but we do offer a call ahead wait list, so if you’d like to call us when you’re on your way, we can go ahead and put your name down.”
